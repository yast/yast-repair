Hi MCS,
There are several possibilities for your CDROM mounting problem.  I am
running RedHat 6.1.

Try the command 'dmesg' to see what your CDROM listing is during the
boot process, here are a few lines from my dmesg output:
hda: Maxtor 91152D8, 10991MB w/256kB Cache, CHS=1401/255/63
hdb: Maxtor 91000D8, 9543MB w/512kB Cache, CHS=1216/255/63
hdd: ATAPI 32X CD-ROM drive, 256kB Cache
Uniform CDROM driver Revision: 2.56
Floppy drive(s): fd0 is 1.44M
FDC 0 is a post-1991 82077

So my system recognizes my CDROM as being at /dev/hdd.
Taking a look at my /dev directory shows:
[piller@piller1 /dev]$ ls -l cdrom
lrwxrwxrwx   1 root     root            3 May 30 21:33 cdrom -> hdd

So there is a symbolic link for my system from /dev/cdrom -> /dev/hdd.

Next, you need to make sure that your /mnt/cdrom directory exists on
your system.

Is your mount command working at all, can you mount/umount your floppy? 
What do you see when you enter 'mount' at the command line?

Is your CDROM listed in your /etc/fstab file?

As for your question about linux kernels, the one with the smp suffix
means that the kernel is for a symmetric multi processor machine (a dual
cpu system, or quad, or...).

Good Luck,
Chip
