Newsgroups: comp.os.linux.help

hello list,

i was trying to disk dump images onto a floppy when all of a sudden my
floppy stopped working. fdformat works but it gives me this error:

bash$ fdformat /dev/fd0
Double-sided, 80 tracks, 18 sec/track. Total capacity 1440 kB.
Formatting ... done
Verifying ... Read: : Input/output error
Problem reading cylinder 0, expected 18432, read -1

when i type in mdir it gives me this:

bash$ mdir
plain_io: Input/output error
init A: could not read boot sector
Cannot initialize 'A:'

when i try to mount the /dev/fd0 drive it gives me this:

bash$ mount /dev/fd0 /mnt
/dev/fd0: Input/output error
mount: you must specify the filesystem type

i specified vfat, ext, ext2, ufs, and msdos to no avail. it does not
work.

i dont understand what happened and i hope i didnt damage my floppy
drive.
the "dd" command i ran was this:

dd if=/boot.img of=/dev/fd0

i am running redhat 5.2.

any help would be greatly appreciated,

phil

It is more likely that the floppy is bad instead of the drive. Try
another floppy and see if this will work.

  fdformat /dev/fd0H1440

And:  dd if=boot.img of=/dev/fd0 bs=1440k
