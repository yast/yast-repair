Rescue-System: research

SuSE Support Database sdb.suse.de

  • Number of articles: approx. 1600
  • Number of relevant articles: approx. 30
  • Method of research: chosing articles with relevant captions, searched the whole list of articles (http://sdb.suse.de/en/sdb/html/inhalt.html) for important keywords (Keywords).
  • Finished on 2001/Jan/26
  • Many auxiliary articles found for several groups. The articles describe a problematic situation with all its symptoms and provide a solution in most cases. These results found a groundwork for the further research.

Newsgroup: comp.os.linux.*

  • Number of articles: approx. 199,000
  • Number of relevant articles: approx. ?
  • Method of research: searched in the newsgroups for important keywords (Keywords).

1. Search

  • Search for "repair OR rescue" in comp.os.linux.*. (Search)
  • Relevant groups:
    • comp.os.linux.misc
    • comp.os.linux.setup
    • comp.os.linux.hardware
    • comp.os.linux.powerpc
    • comp.os.linux.alpha
    • comp.os.linux.*
  • Number of articles: the first 100 articles dated from 2000/Aug/23 to 2001/Jan/26
  • Number of relevant articles: 16
  • Most of the articles treated newbie problems about using a rescue disk, booting the system, using LILO and integrating two or more operating systems in one computer, especially the boot procedure.

2. Search

  • Search for "corrupt OR defect OR fail" in comp.os.linux.*. (Search)
  • Relevant groups:

    • comp.os.linux.setup

    • comp.os.linux.networking
    • comp.os.linux.*
  • Number of articles: the first 100 articles dated from 2000/Aug/17 to 2001/Mar/03
  • Number of relevant articles: approx. 25, listed 12
  • A lot of newbie questions, a lot of articles about which OS to use: Linux or Windows, some dual boot problems, some problems with logging in, a lot of problems with ext2fs and corrupted partition tables. A wide area of questions was found.
